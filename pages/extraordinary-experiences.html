    Canada - Newfoundland and Labrador

    No matter where you find yourself in Newfoundland and Labrador, you’ll feel so very, very far way. This place does that to you. It sweeps you off your feet and keeps you there for as long as you want. The land itself is like no other - cliffs and barrens, bights and guts - around every corner there’s the unexpected.

    Your adventure will lead you along ancient footpaths to deep fjords, towering cliffs and sub-arctic barrens, through lush inland forests, and over the earth’s mantle. Take a sea kayak to caves, inlets, and secluded beaches along routes protected by offshore islands, and view the land from the sea. Immerse yourself in a place where 10,000 whales get front row seats to go people watching, where icebergs drift along ancient shores, where there isn’t a rock, cave or cliff without a story attached.

    Sweden - Jämtland Härjedalen

    The region of Jämtland Härjedalen is situated in central Sweden, has a unique flora and fauna and is often referred to as "the lungs of Europe".

    In addition to the clean air, it is well known for its accessible wilderness, superb homemade foods, the contrasts of the midnight sun and the winter darkness and its five clearly defined seasons.

    In wintertime skiing is the most popular activity in the region, but the summertime showcases the regions picturesque landscapes in which you can enjoy a hike with reindeers, cycle through small villages and down rugged mountains, visit the beautiful Storsjön lake and even meet the resident lake monster, The Storsjöodjuret... if you’re lucky!

    Ecuador

    For its size, Ecuador has an especially impressive array of interesting sites and cities. The high Andes beckon with stunning vistas, colourful indigenous markets, and scenic colonial neighbourhoods, where Ecuador’s “Avenue of the Volcanoes” is sure to inject you with your first dose of adventure. The eastern lowlands offer an exciting Amazonian experience where you can visit exotic wildlife species and the ancient Huaorani tribe of the rainforest.

    Then there are the Galapagos Islands. Located more than 900 kilometers from the coast, these fabled islands are part of Ecuadorian territory and the jewel of Ecuador. The Galapagos are on the bucket list of nearly every world traveller and those who make their way to this volcanic archipelago find their expectations surpassed by their experience.
